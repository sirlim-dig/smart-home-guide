Robot Vacuum Buying Guide: What Actually Matters

Suction Power

Suction is measured in Pascals (Pa). For hardwood and tile, 2,000 Pa is sufficient. For carpet, you want 4,000+ Pa. For deep carpet or heavy pet hair, 7,000+ Pa makes a noticeable difference. Most premium robots in 2026 offer 5,000‚Äì10,000 Pa.

Navigation Technology

LiDAR is the gold standard ‚Äî it creates accurate room maps and navigates efficiently in straight rows. Camera-based navigation (used by some budget models) works but is slower and less precise. Bump-and-go navigation (old Roombas) is obsolete ‚Äî avoid it.

To Mop or Not to Mop?

Robot mops in 2026 handle daily maintenance mopping well but won't replace a deep clean with a traditional mop. If you have mostly hard floors and want to reduce cleaning frequency, a combo vacuum-mop is worth the premium. If you have mostly carpet, save the money and get a dedicated vacuum.

Auto-Empty Docks

An auto-empty dock is the single best quality-of-life feature in a robot vacuum. Without one, you're emptying a tiny dustbin every 1‚Äì2 cleans. With one, you empty a larger bag every 4‚Äì8 weeks. We consider this essential for a "set it and forget it" experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I run my robot vacuum?

Daily or every other day for best results. Robot vacuums work best with frequent, lighter cleaning rather than weekly deep cleans. Most homes benefit from daily runs in high-traffic areas and every-other-day runs elsewhere.

Do robot vacuums work on thick carpet?

Yes, but you need adequate suction. Look for 5,000+ Pa and rubber brushes (not bristle). Low-profile robots may also struggle to climb onto very thick rugs ‚Äî check the climb height spec (15mm+ is good).

Can robot vacuums go over thresholds and rugs?

Most modern robot vacuums can climb thresholds up to 20mm (about 3/4 inch). For higher thresholds, you may need small ramps. Fringe-edged rugs can be problematic ‚Äî tuck the fringe under or use no-go zones.

Are expensive robot vacuums worth it?

For most people, a mid-range robot ($500‚Äì$800) offers the best value. Premium models ($1,000+) are worth it if you have a large home, pets that shed heavily, or you want the absolute best obstacle avoidance and mopping. Budget models ($300‚Äì$500) are fine for smaller homes without pets.
