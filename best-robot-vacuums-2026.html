What to Look for in a Robot Vacuum in 2026

Navigation

LiDAR is our strong recommendation. Spinning laser creates precise maps, cleans in neat rows, rarely misses spots. Camera-based (vSLAM) is nearly as good but struggles in the dark. Bump-and-wander is only for sub-$200 models.

Suction Power

Hardwood: 2,000 Pa is fine. Medium carpet: 5,000+ Pa. Thick carpet/pet hair: 8,000+ Pa. Above 10,000 Pa is largely marketing ‚Äî real gains plateau around 8K‚Äì10K Pa.

Mopping

Modern combo robots use vibrating or rotating mops with pressurized water. The best auto-lift the mop for carpet. If you have mixed floors, a combo can replace both a vacuum and a Swiffer.

Self-empty docks vacuum debris into a bag lasting 30‚Äì60 days. Premium docks also refill water, wash mop pads with hot water, dry them, and add cleaning solution automatically. You literally don't touch the robot for a month.

Robot Vacuum Maintenance Tips

üîß

Clean brushes weekly. Hair wraps around the main and side brushes. A quick cut-and-pull keeps performance up.

üîß

Replace filters every 2‚Äì3 months. Clogged filters reduce suction dramatically.

üîß

Clean sensors monthly. A microfiber cloth over the LiDAR and cliff sensors prevents navigation errors.

üîß

Check the wheels. Hair and debris wrap around axles, causing drag.

üîß

Empty the dock bag monthly. A full bag causes suction loss and overheating.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a robot vacuum replace my regular vacuum?
For daily maintenance, yes. For deep cleaning (stairs, upholstery, high corners), you'll still want a handheld or stick vacuum. Most households use their manual vacuum 80% less after getting a good robot.
Are robot vacuums safe for pets?
Yes. Modern robots coexist with pets fine. Models with camera-based obstacle avoidance (like the Roborock S9 MaxV) identify and avoid pet waste ‚Äî critical for pet owners.
How loud are robot vacuums?
Most produce 55‚Äì70 dB at standard suction ‚Äî roughly the volume of a conversation. Turbo modes are louder (70‚Äì75 dB). Schedule cleaning while you're out if noise bothers you.
