Smart Lock Buyer's Guide: What to Look For

Retrofit vs. Full Replacement

Retrofit locks (August, Level) replace only the inside of your existing deadbolt. You keep your keys and exterior hardware. They're easier to install, renter-friendly, and don't change your door's appearance. Full replacement locks (Yale, Schlage, Ultraloq, Kwikset, Wyze) replace the entire deadbolt assembly. They typically include a keypad for code entry, which retrofit locks usually don't.

Our recommendation: if you rent or want the easiest install, go retrofit. If you own your home and want a keypad for guest access, go full replacement.

Connectivity: Wi-Fi vs. Bluetooth vs. Thread

Wi-Fi: Lets you control the lock from anywhere via the internet. Required for remote access, Alexa/Google integration, and smart home automations. Uses more battery. Bluetooth: Only works when your phone is nearby. No remote access. Uses less battery. Thread: Low-power mesh networking. Fast response, works with Matter-compatible hubs. Best balance of battery life and functionality.

For most people, Wi-Fi is the minimum you should look for. Thread is a bonus for future-proofing.

Security Ratings Explained

ANSI/BHMA grades rate a lock's physical security:

  • Grade 1: Highest residential security (Schlage Encode Plus). Withstands 800,000 cycles and 10 strikes.
  • Grade 2: Standard residential (most locks on this list). Withstands 400,000 cycles and 5 strikes.
  • Grade 3: Light residential. Avoid for exterior doors.

For a front door, Grade 2 is the minimum. Grade 1 is better if security is a top priority.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are smart locks safe? Can they be hacked?

Smart locks from reputable brands use AES-128 or AES-256 encryption ‚Äî the same standard used in banking. The realistic security risk isn't hacking (which requires specialized equipment and proximity), it's weak passwords on your smart home account. Use a strong, unique password and enable two-factor authentication.

What happens if the battery dies?

Every lock on this list provides low-battery warnings weeks in advance. If the battery does die, most locks have a 9V battery terminal on the exterior for emergency power, plus a physical key backup. You won't be locked out.

Can I install a smart lock on my apartment?

Yes ‚Äî with a retrofit lock like August or Level, you don't modify the exterior hardware or change the key. When you move out, remove the smart lock and reinstall the original interior mechanism. Takes 10 minutes. Check your lease, but most landlords won't even notice.

Do smart locks work with smart home automations?

Absolutely. Common automations: lock all doors at bedtime, unlock when you arrive home, send an alert when a guest code is used, turn on lights when the front door unlocks at night. Wi-Fi or Thread connectivity is required for these integrations.
