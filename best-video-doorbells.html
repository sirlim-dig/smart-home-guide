Video Doorbell Buying Guide

Battery vs Wired

Battery doorbells install in 10 minutes with no wiring ‚Äî perfect for renters or anyone who doesn't have existing doorbell wiring. Wired doorbells connect to your home's doorbell transformer for continuous power, meaning they never need charging and can record 24/7. If you have existing wiring, wired is better. If you don't, battery is your only option (and it works great).

The Subscription Question

Most video doorbells are severely limited without a subscription. Ring without Ring Protect gives you live view only ‚Äî no recorded video. Factor in 3‚Äì5 years of subscription costs when comparing total price. A $100 Ring + 3 years of Ring Protect = $220 total. A $80 Reolink with local storage = $80 total. The "cheap" doorbell isn't always cheap.

What About Package Detection?

Package detection alerts you specifically when a delivery arrives (not just generic motion). Ring and Arlo both offer this with their subscriptions. It's genuinely useful during holiday shopping season and for anyone who gets frequent deliveries. If package theft is a concern, prioritize doorbells with this feature.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I install a video doorbell in an apartment?

Battery-powered doorbells can be mounted with adhesive strips or a simple bracket ‚Äî no drilling required. However, check your lease first. Some apartments don't allow exterior modifications. Consider an indoor mount pointing at the door as an alternative.

Do video doorbells work in cold weather?

Yes, but battery life decreases significantly in cold temperatures. Below freezing, expect 30‚Äì50% less battery life. Wired doorbells are unaffected by temperature. If you live in a cold climate, wired power or a solar panel charger is recommended.

How fast do video doorbell notifications arrive?

Ring typically delivers notifications in 3‚Äì5 seconds. Nest and Arlo are slightly slower at 5‚Äì8 seconds. Live view connection takes an additional 2‚Äì5 seconds on all platforms. These delays mean you might miss talking to a delivery person if you're not quick ‚Äî but the recorded footage captures everything.
